# Wallet Evaluation Criteria

You are evaluating Polymarket esports wallets to decide whether they should be added to or removed from a copy-trading CSV. Your goal is to find **consistent, skilled bettors** — not one-hit wonders, not arbers, not whales scalping guaranteed outcomes.

## What Makes a Good Wallet

- **Consistency over raw numbers.** A wallet with 8% ROI spread across 40 markets is better than a wallet with 50% ROI driven by one lucky bet. Look at how P&L is distributed across markets — the more evenly spread, the better.
- **Real edge, not mechanical arb.** Good wallets find mispriced lines and take positions with genuine risk. They're buying outcomes at prices that reflect actual prediction skill, not just exploiting guaranteed math.
- **Minimum 20 unique markets/events.** Anything less and there isn't enough data to distinguish skill from luck.
- **Sustained performance over time.** If the wallet's P&L chart looks like one spike followed by flatline or decline, it's probably a one-hit wonder. Look for steady upward trajectories or at least multiple distinct winning periods.

## Instant Red Flags (Remove or Do Not Add)

- **Guaranteed outcome scalping.** Wallet buys outcomes priced at 97¢+ in large size, collecting 1-3% on near-certain results. These wallets are profitable but uncopyable — we can't get the same fills at our size.
- **Excessive both-sides hedging.** Wallet frequently buys both sides of the same market when total cost dips below $1.00. Doing this occasionally is fine — most good traders hedge sometimes. But if it's a core part of their strategy or happening on a large percentage of their trades, it's a problem because slippage on our end destroys the margin.
- **P&L concentration.** If 90%+ of a wallet's total P&L comes from just 1-2 markets, it's a one-hit wonder regardless of how good the headline ROI looks.
- **Low trade count with huge sizes.** A wallet that has placed 5 massive bets is gambling, not trading. We need volume to trust the signal.

## What Doesn't Matter

- **Pre-match vs live betting.** Both are fine.
- **Bot vs manual trading.** Doesn't affect our decision.
- **Specific game preference.** CS2, LoL, Dota, Valorant — the game itself isn't a factor. The trading behavior is what matters.

## How to Think About Removal

Don't remove a wallet just because it had a bad week. Drawdowns happen. Remove when:
- The wallet's behavior has fundamentally changed (was a grinder, now only takes 1-2 huge bets)
- A pattern has emerged that matches a known red flag (shifted to arb-style trading)
- Extended underperformance with no recovery signal (3+ weeks of consistent losses with no winning markets mixed in)
- The original thesis for adding the wallet no longer holds based on new data
